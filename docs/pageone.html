What is the Bus Open Data Service

The Bus Open Data Service is a digital service that will enable bus passengers to enjoy a much more informed bus journey.

For bus services within scope they will need to publish their:

  • Fares Data
  • Timetables Data
  • Location Data



The deadline for operators to upload data are as follows:

  • Basic Fares Data - 7 January 2021
  • GPS Data - 7 January 2021
  • Basic Timetables Data - 31 December 2020




Does my services fall within scope

This legislation covers all local bus services in England and outside of London:

  • Cross-border services (so those that run in and outside of London and Wales or London and England
  • Point-to-point tour journeys (as opposed to full tour)

However, some services may not need to comply with the legislation under section 19 or 22. This includes:

  • Coach services
  • Tour Services
  • Unregistered dedicated school services
  • Voluntarily registered and dedicated school services

If you require a scope check please email here and request for a scope check.


What is the enforcement policy

It is the operators legal obligation to comply, meaning that in the case of non-compliance there may be enforcement.


How to become compliant - A step to step guide to become compliant


Here is a bullet point list for operators to upload their data:

  1. Register here if you do not have an account.
  2. Reset your password here if you have forgotten it.
  3. Log in here if you already have an account.


    4. Now you have an account, you will need to obtain your data.

  4. Download the free TransXchange tool here. You can find more information here.
  5. Use the free Create Fares Data Service to create your fares information
  6. If you do not have a tracking device, you can find a supplier here. If you are unsure as to whether you already have a tracking device contact your supplier.


    7. Now you have your datasets, you will need to log into your account. Once you have logged in, there will be a clear step to step direction to help you upload your datasets in your portal.


  7. Agents - if you used an agent then you will need to go into your account and add them into your portal. How much an agent helps you is dependent on the agreement between the operator and the agent. Not all agents offer the same services so please check with them if you are unsure.
  8. Suppliers - if you do not wish to use the free tools, there are other services that are available to you which you can find here.
